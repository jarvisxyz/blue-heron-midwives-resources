# Resource 2: Birth Plan Template

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/grh-pre-admit-forms.html  
**Resource Title:** Birth Plan Template  
**Session:** GRH Forms (Resource 2 of 5)

## Summary

This resource provides a template for creating a birth plan to share with Grand River Hospital staff.

### Key Points

- **Birth Plan Sections:**
  - **Labor preferences:** Movement, positions, food/drink
  - **Monitoring preferences:** Frequency of fetal heart monitoring
  - **Pain management:** Preferences for medications and non-pharmacological methods
  - **Interventions:** Preferences for induction, augmentation, episiotomy
  - **Birth environment:** Lighting, music, who's present
  - **Immediate postpartum:** Skin-to-skin, delayed cord clamping, breastfeeding

- **Template Format:**
  - Keep to 1-2 pages
  - Use clear, simple language
  - Prioritize preferences
  - Include flexibility statement
  - Share with birth team

- **Hospital-Specific Considerations:**
  - Review hospital policies
  - Understand available options
  - Discuss with midwife
  - Adapt to hospital resources

- **Distribution:**
  - Provide copies to:
    - Midwife
    - Partner/support person
    - Hospital (if required)
    - Keep personal copy

- **Flexibility Statement:**
  - Include statement that plan may change
  - Prioritize healthy outcome
  - Trust medical team when needed

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Improves communication with hospital staff
- Reduces anxiety about birth
- Helps partners understand preferences
- May reduce unnecessary interventions
- Supports informed decision-making
- Enhances birth experience

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Birth plans are not guarantees:** Medical needs come first
- **Be flexible:** Labour is unpredictable
- **Discuss with midwife:** Ensure preferences are feasible
- **Share copies:** With birth team and hospital
- **Focus on priorities:** Don't get stuck on details
- **Revisit and update:** As pregnancy progresses
