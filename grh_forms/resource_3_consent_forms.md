# Resource 3: Consent Forms

**Resource Title:** Consent Forms  
**Session:** GRH Forms (Resource 3 of 5)

## Summary

This resource covers consent forms required for hospital procedures during labour and birth.

### Key Points

- **Types of Consent Forms:**
  - **General hospital admission consent**
  - **Blood transfusion consent**
  - **Surgical consent (if C-section needed)**
  - **Medication consent**
  - **Procedural consents**

- **Informed Consent Process:**
  - Information about procedure
  - Benefits and risks explained
  - Alternatives discussed
  - Time to consider decision
  - Right to refuse treatment

- **Common Procedures Requiring Consent:**
  - Cesarean section
  - Episiotomy
  - Forceps or vacuum delivery
  - Blood transfusion
  - Medications (antibiotics, pain relief)

- **Your Rights:**
  - Right to complete information
  - Right to ask questions
  - Right to time to decide
  - Right to refuse treatment
  - Right to change mind

- **Documentation:**
  - Forms must be signed
  - Copies provided to patient
  - Records kept in medical file
  - Can request copies later

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Protects patient rights
- Ensures informed decision-making
- Reduces legal complications
- Enhances trust in healthcare team
- Supports autonomy
- Improves quality of care

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Read carefully:** Before signing
- **Ask questions:** Until you understand
- **Time to decide:** Don't rush consent
- **Right to refuse:** You can decline procedures
- **Emergency exceptions:** Some procedures may proceed without consent in emergencies
- **Resources:** Patient advocates available
