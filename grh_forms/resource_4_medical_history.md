## Summary

This resource covers the medical history questionnaire required for hospital admission, including what information is needed and why it's important.

### Key Points

- **Personal Medical History:**
  - Chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension, etc.)
  - Previous surgeries
  - Hospitalizations
  - Current medications
  - Allergies (medications, foods, environmental)

- **Obstetric History:**
  - Previous pregnancies and births
  - Complications in previous pregnancies
  - Birth methods (vaginal, C-section)
  - Baby outcomes
  - Postpartum complications

- **Family History:**
  - Genetic conditions
  - Multiple births
  - Pregnancy complications in family
  - Blood disorders

- **Social History:**
  - Substance use (alcohol, drugs, tobacco)
  - Occupation and work environment
  - Support system
  - Living situation

- **Current Pregnancy:**
  - Prenatal care history
  - Test results
  - Current symptoms
  - Fetal movement patterns

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Essential for safe medical care
- Identifies potential complications
- Guides treatment decisions
- Improves hospital preparedness
- Reduces medical errors
- Enhances patient safety

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Complete accurately:** Incomplete information may affect care
- **Update if changes:** Inform hospital of new information
- **Confidentiality:** Information is protected
- **Ask questions:** If unsure what to include
- **Bring records:** If seeing different providers
- **Resources:** Midwife can help complete questionnaire
