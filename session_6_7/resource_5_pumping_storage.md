# Resource 5: Pumping and Storage

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-6-7.html  
**Resource Title:** Pumping and Storage  
**Session:** Session 6-7 (Resource 5 of 12)

## Summary

This resource provides guidance on breast pumping, milk storage, and handling breast milk safely.

### Key Points

- **When to Pump:**
  - Returning to work
  - Separation from baby
  - Building milk stash
  - Relieving engorgement
  - Feeding baby expressed milk

- **Pump Types:**
  - **Manual:** Portable, less expensive
  - **Electric:** Single or double
  - **Hospital-grade:** For building supply
  - **Choose:** Based on needs and budget

- **Milk Storage Guidelines:**
  - **Room temperature:** 4 hours
  - **Refrigerator:** 4 days
  - **Freezer:** 6 months ideal, 12 months acceptable
  - **Containers:** Breast milk bags or BPA-free containers

- **Thawing and Warming:**
  - Thaw in refrigerator overnight
  - Warm in warm water
  - Never microwave
  - Use within 24 hours after thawing
  - Don't refreeze

- **Hygiene:**
  - Wash hands before pumping
  - Clean pump parts regularly
  - Label milk with date
  - Store properly

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Enables feeding when separated from baby
- Supports working parents
- Allows partner to participate in feeding
- Reduces stress of separation
- Maintains milk supply
- Provides flexibility

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't microwave:** Creates hot spots
- **Label everything:** Date and amount
- **Follow storage guidelines:** Safety first
- **Clean equipment:** Prevents infection
- **Discard unused:** Don't save for later
- **Resources:** Pumping support available
