## Summary

This resource covers newborn sleep patterns, safe sleep practices, and strategies for helping baby sleep safely.

### Key Points

- **Newborn Sleep Patterns:**
  - Sleeps 16-18 hours daily
  - Sleeps in 2-4 hour stretches
  - No day/night distinction initially
  - Irregular patterns normal

- **Safe Sleep Guidelines:**
  - **Back to sleep:** Always place on back
  - **Firm surface:** Mattress with fitted sheet
  - **No soft items:** No pillows, blankets, toys in crib
  - **Room-sharing:** Baby in parents' room, not bed
  - **No overheating:** Dress appropriately

- **SIDS Prevention:**
  - Back sleeping reduces risk
  - Avoid smoke exposure
  - Breastfeeding reduces risk
  - Pacifier use may help
  - Immunizations important

- **Helping Baby Sleep:**
  - Establish day/night routine
  - Watch for sleep cues
  - Soothing techniques
  - White noise may help
  - Don't expect long stretches initially

- **Parent Sleep:**
  - Sleep when baby sleeps
  - Share night duties with partner
  - Accept help from others
  - Don't worry about housework

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces SIDS risk
- Ensures baby's safety
- Supports healthy development
- Reduces parental anxiety
- Enhances family functioning
- Promotes healthy sleep habits

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Never sleep train newborn:** They need to feed frequently
- **Back to sleep:** Always, even for naps
- **No bed-sharing:** Increases SIDS risk
- **Safe sleep environment:** Follow guidelines
- **Don't worry about schedule:** Newborns don't have one
- **Resources:** Safe sleep education available
