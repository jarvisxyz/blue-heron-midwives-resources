## Summary

This resource covers common breastfeeding challenges and how to address them effectively.

### Key Points

- **Common Challenges:**
  - **Engorgement:** Overfull, painful breasts
  - **Mastitis:** Breast infection
  - **Low supply concerns:** Often unfounded
  - **Nipple pain and damage:** Usually latch-related
  - **Baby refusing breast:** Temporary issue
  - **Cluster feeding:** Normal pattern

- **Engorgement Management:**
  - Feed frequently
  - Warm compress before feeding
  - Cold compress after
  - Hand express if needed
  - Supportive bra

- **Mastitis:**
  - **Signs:** Red, painful area, fever, flu-like symptoms
  - **Treatment:** Continue breastfeeding, antibiotics if bacterial
  - **Prevention:** Good latch, frequent feeding
  - **When to see doctor:** If fever or worsening

- **Low Supply Concerns:**
  - **Most concerns unfounded:** Supply usually adequate
  - **Signs of adequate intake:** Wet diapers, weight gain
  - **Increasing supply:** Feed more frequently
  - **Support:** Lactation consultant

- **Baby Refusing Breast:**
  - **Causes:** Bottle preference, illness, distraction
  - **Solutions:** Try different positions, skin-to-skin
  - **Patience:** Often temporary

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Helps overcome early challenges
- Supports breastfeeding success
- Reduces early cessation
- Enhances comfort
- Improves confidence
- Reduces need for formula

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Mastitis requires treatment:** See doctor if symptoms
- **Don't stop breastfeeding:** Continue to empty breast
- **Seek help:** Lactation consultants available
- **Most challenges temporary:** With support
- **Don't blame yourself:** Many factors involved
- **Resources:** Breastfeeding support groups
