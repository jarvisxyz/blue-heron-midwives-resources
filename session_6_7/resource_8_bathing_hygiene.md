# Resource 8: Bathing and Hygiene

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-6-7.html  
**Resource Title:** Bathing and Hygiene  
**Session:** Session 6-7 (Resource 8 of 12)

## Summary

This resource provides guidance on newborn bathing, umbilical cord care, and general hygiene practices.

### Key Points

- **First Bath:**
  - Can wait 24 hours if possible
  - Sponge bath initially
  - Keep baby warm
  - Use minimal products

- **Bathing Techniques:**
  - **Sponge bathing:** First few weeks
  - **Tub bathing:** When cord falls off
  - **Water temperature:** 37-38°C (98-100°F)
  - **Support head and neck**
  - **Keep room warm**

- **Umbilical Cord Care:**
  - Keep clean and dry
  - Fold diaper below cord
  - Air dry when possible
  - Cord falls off in 1-2 weeks
  - Watch for infection signs

- **Skin Care:**
  - Use mild, fragrance-free products
  - Moisturize if dry
  - Diaper rash prevention
  - Avoid powders

- **Nail Care:**
  - Trim regularly to prevent scratching
  - Use baby nail clippers or file
  - Trim when baby sleeping

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Maintains baby's health and comfort
- Prevents infection
- Establishes hygiene routines
- Enhances bonding during care
- Reduces parental anxiety
- Promotes healthy development

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Never leave baby unattended:** In bath or on changing table
- **Watch cord for infection:** Redness, swelling, foul odor
- **Water temperature:** Test before placing baby in
- **Use minimal products:** Baby skin is sensitive
- **Take your time:** Bathing doesn't need to be rushed
- **Resources:** New parent classes teach bathing
