## Summary

This resource provides information about formula feeding options, preparation, and considerations for parents who choose or need to use formula.

### Key Points

- **Formula Types:**
  - **Cow's milk-based:** Most common
  - **Soy-based:** For lactose intolerance
  - **Hydrolyzed:** For protein sensitivity
  - **Special formulas:** For specific medical needs

- **Formula Preparation:**
  - Wash hands thoroughly
  - Clean bottles and nipples
  - Follow mixing instructions precisely
  - Use safe water
  - Test temperature before feeding

- **Feeding Guidelines:**
  - **Newborns:** 60-90ml every 2-3 hours
  - **Follow baby's cues:** Don't force feeding
  - **Burping:** Important to prevent gas
  - **Pacing:** Allow baby to control pace

- **When Formula May Be Needed:**
  - Medical reasons
  - Parent choice
  - Insufficient milk supply
  - Adoption or fostering
  - Combination feeding

- **Cost and Access:**
  - Formula can be expensive
  - Some programs provide free formula
  - Check eligibility for assistance
  - Compare brands

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Provides nutrition when breastfeeding not possible
- Supports parent choice
- Allows partner participation
- Ensures baby receives adequate nutrition
- Reduces stress about feeding
- Supports family bonding

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Formula is safe:** Many healthy babies fed with formula
- **Don't feel guilty:** Feeding method doesn't determine love
- **Follow preparation guidelines:** Safety important
- **Burp frequently:** Prevents discomfort
- **Storage:** Prepared formula has limits
- **Resources:** Lactation consultants can help with combination feeding
