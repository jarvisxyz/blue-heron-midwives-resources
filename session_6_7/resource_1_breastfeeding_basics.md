## Summary

This resource provides foundational information about breastfeeding, including benefits, techniques, and initial challenges.

### Key Points

- **Benefits of Breastfeeding:**
  - **For baby:** Optimal nutrition, immune protection, bonding
  - **For parent:** Faster uterine recovery, bonding, convenience
  - **Long-term:** Reduced risk of certain diseases for both

- **Getting Started:**
  - Skin-to-skin contact immediately after birth
  - Feed on demand (8-12 times per day)
  - Watch for hunger cues
  - Don't worry about schedule initially
  - Seek help early if struggling

- **Feeding Cues:**
  - Rooting (turning head, opening mouth)
  - Sucking on hands
  - Smacking lips
  - Crying is late sign

- **Frequency and Duration:**
  - Newborns feed 8-12 times daily
  - Each feeding 10-45 minutes
  - Cluster feeding common in evenings
  - Growth spurts increase feeding

- **What to Expect:**
  - Frequent feeding initially
  - Some discomfort at first
  - Engorgement around day 3-5
  - Establishing supply takes time

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Provides optimal nutrition for baby
- Supports immune system development
- Enhances parent-infant bonding
- Reduces risk of infections
- Supports healthy growth
- Convenience for parents

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Seek help early:** Lactation consultants available
- **Pain is not normal:** Adjust latch if painful
- **Don't supplement without reason:** Breast milk is sufficient
- **Hydration and nutrition:** Important for milk production
- **Resources available:** Support groups, helplines
- **Exclusive breastfeeding:** Recommended for first 6 months (Health Canada)
