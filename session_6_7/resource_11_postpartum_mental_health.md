## Summary

This resource covers postpartum mental health, including baby blues, postpartum depression, and when to seek help.

### Key Points

- **Baby Blues:**
  - Affects 70-80% of new parents
  - Begins 2-3 days after birth
  - Lasts up to 2 weeks
  - Symptoms: Mood swings, crying, anxiety
  - Self-limiting, no treatment needed

- **Postpartum Depression (PPD):**
  - Affects 10-15% of new parents
  - Can begin during pregnancy or after birth
  - Lasts longer than 2 weeks
  - Symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest, guilt, thoughts of harm

- **Postpartum Anxiety:**
  - Excessive worry about baby
  - Panic attacks
  - Intrusive thoughts
  - Physical symptoms

- **Postpartum Psychosis (Rare):**
  - Emergency situation
  - Hallucinations, delusions
  - Thoughts of harming self or baby
  - Requires immediate medical attention

- **Treatment Options:**
  - Psychotherapy (CBT, interpersonal)
  - Medication (many safe while breastfeeding)
  - Support groups
  - Lifestyle changes
  - Peer support

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Supports parent mental health
- Enhances parent-infant bonding
- Improves family functioning
- Reduces risk of long-term mental health issues
- Supports healthy child development
- Improves quality of life

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Emergency help:** If thoughts of self-harm, call 911 or crisis line immediately
- **Don't wait:** Early treatment prevents worsening
- **Partner mental health:** Also affected (5-10%)
- **Medication decisions:** Discuss with provider
- **Recovery is possible:** Most parents recover with treatment
- **Resources available:** Help is accessible
