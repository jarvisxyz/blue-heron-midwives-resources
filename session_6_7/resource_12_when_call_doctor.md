# Resource 12: When to Call the Doctor

## Summary

This resource outlines when to contact healthcare providers for newborn and postpartum concerns.

### Key Points

- **Newborn Warning Signs:**
  - Fever over 38°C (100.4°F)
  - Poor feeding or refusal to feed
  - Lethargy or excessive sleepiness
  - Fewer than 6 wet diapers daily after day 5
  - Jaundice spreading to arms and legs
  - Breathing difficulties
  - Blue or grey color

- **Postpartum Warning Signs:**
  - Heavy bleeding (soaking pad in hour)
  - Fever over 38°C (100.4°F)
  - Severe headache
  - Chest pain or shortness of breath
  - Thoughts of harming self or baby
  - Severe mood changes
  - Wound infection (redness, swelling, drainage)

- **Breastfeeding Concerns:**
  - Severe nipple pain or damage
  - Signs of mastitis (fever, red area)
  - Concerns about milk supply
  - Baby not gaining weight

- **When to Go to Emergency:**
  - Severe bleeding
  - Signs of stroke (sudden severe headache, vision changes)
  - Thoughts of self-harm or harming baby
  - Severe breathing difficulties
  - Seizures

- **Contact Information:**
  - Keep midwife/doctor number handy
  - After-hours contact procedures
  - Emergency department location
  - Crisis lines saved in phone

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Ensures timely medical attention
- Prevents complications
- Reduces anxiety about when to seek help
- Supports informed decision-making
- Maintains continuity of care
- Provides peace of mind

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Emergency situations:** Call 911 if severe symptoms
- **Don't wait:** Better to call with concerns
- **Trust your instincts:** You know your baby best
- **After-hours:** Providers typically available 24/7
- **Emergency department:** Go if unable to reach provider and concerned
- **Documentation:** Keep contact information accessible
