## Summary

This resource covers physical recovery after birth, including healing, self-care, and when to seek medical attention.

### Key Points

- **Normal Postpartum Changes:**
  - **Bleeding (lochia):** 4-6 weeks, decreases over time
  - **Uterine contractions:** Afterpains, especially with breastfeeding
  - **Breast changes:** Engorgement, milk coming in
  - **Hormonal changes:** Mood fluctuations
  - **Fatigue:** Very common

- **Perineal Care (Vaginal Birth):**
  - Keep area clean and dry
  - Use peri bottle for washing
  - Ice packs for swelling
  - Sitz baths if helpful
  - Pain management as needed

- **C-Section Recovery:**
  - Keep incision clean and dry
  - Watch for infection signs
  - Pain management
  - Gradual increase in activity
  - No heavy lifting

- **Self-Care:**
  - Rest when possible
  - Accept help from others
  - Eat nutritious meals
  - Stay hydrated
  - Gentle movement as able

- **When to Call Doctor:**
  - Heavy bleeding (soaking pad in hour)
  - Fever over 38°C
  - Severe pain
  - Signs of infection
  - Thoughts of self-harm

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Supports physical healing
- Reduces complications
- Enhances recovery
- Improves quality of life
- Supports ability to care for baby
- Promotes long-term health

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Recovery takes time:** 6+ weeks for full recovery
- **Don't rush:** Listen to your body
- **Ask for help:** Household tasks are exhausting
- **Mental health:** Watch for postpartum depression
- **Follow-up appointments:** Attend all scheduled visits
- **Resources:** Postpartum support available
