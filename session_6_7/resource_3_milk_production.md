## Summary

This resource explains how breast milk production works, factors that affect supply, and how to maintain adequate milk production.

### Key Points

- **How Milk Production Works:**
  - **Prolactin:** Hormone that makes milk
  - **Oxytocin:** Hormone that releases milk (let-down)
  - **Supply and demand:** More removal = more production
  - **Colostrum:** First milk, high in antibodies
  - **Milk coming in:** Around day 3-5

- **Factors Affecting Supply:**
  - **Frequency of feeding:** More feeding = more milk
  - **Effective removal:** Good latch important
  - **Maternal nutrition and hydration:** Support production
  - **Stress and fatigue:** Can temporarily reduce supply
  - **Medications:** Some affect milk production

- **Maintaining Supply:**
  - Feed frequently (8-12 times daily)
  - Ensure good latch
  - Feed on both sides
  - Avoid unnecessary supplementation
  - Rest and hydration

- **Signs of Adequate Intake:**
  - 6+ wet diapers daily after day 5
  - 3-4+ bowel movements daily
  - Steady weight gain
  - Satisfied after feeding
  - Swallowing sounds during feeding

- **Increasing Supply:**
  - Feed more frequently
  - Ensure effective latch
  - Pump after feeds if needed
  - Skin-to-skin contact
  - Consider galactagogues (consult provider)

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Ensures adequate nutrition for baby
- Supports healthy growth
- Reduces need for formula
- Enhances bonding
- Reduces risk of supplementation
- Supports long-term breastfeeding success

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't worry about "empty" breasts:** Milk is always being made
- **Supply fluctuates:** Normal during growth spurts
- **Supplementation:** Only when medically indicated
- **Seek help:** If concerned about supply
- **Hydration:** Drink to thirst
- **Resources:** Lactation consultants available
