## Summary

This resource covers breathing techniques that can help manage pain and anxiety during labour, including patterns and applications.

### Key Points

- **Early Labour Breathing:**
  - Slow, deep breathing
  - Focus on exhalation
  - Calming and relaxing
  - Helps conserve energy

- **Active Labour Breathing:**
  - Patterned breathing
  - Slightly faster than normal
  - Helps focus and distract from pain
  - Examples: Slow paced, Modified paced

- **Transition Breathing:**
  - Panting or shallow breathing
  - For intense contractions
  - Prevents holding breath
  - Helps push effectively

- **Pushing Breathing:**
  - Open glottis pushing
  - Follow urge to push
  - Breath holding techniques if needed
  - Coordinate with contractions

- **Relaxation Breathing:**
  - Between contractions
  - Reset and recover
  - Reduce tension
  - Prepare for next contraction

- **Practice Techniques:**
  - Learn during pregnancy
  - Practice daily
  - Use with partner
  - Incorporate into birth plan

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces pain perception
- Lowers anxiety and fear
- Conserves energy
- Enhances coping ability
- May reduce need for medical pain relief
- Improves birth experience

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Practice beforehand:** Techniques work better when learned
- **Don't hold breath:** Can reduce oxygen to baby
- **Stay relaxed:** Tension increases pain
- **Adapt to labour:** Different stages need different breathing
- **Support person:** Can help remind you to breathe
