## Summary

This resource explores how massage and touch therapies can help manage labour pain and enhance comfort.

### Key Points

- **Types of Massage:**
  - **Back massage:** Reduces back labour pain
  - **Foot massage:** Relaxing, grounding
  - **Hand massage:** Calming
  - **Shoulder massage:** Releases tension
  - **Counter-pressure:** Firm pressure on specific areas

- **Techniques:**
  - Rhythmic rubbing
  - Circular motions
  - Firm pressure
  - Light stroking
  - Combination techniques

- **When to Use:**
  - During contractions
  - Between contractions for recovery
  - For back labour specifically
  - For relaxation and comfort

- **Partner Involvement:**
  - Learn techniques beforehand
  - Use massage oil
  - Apply firm pressure
  - Follow labouring person's preferences
  - Take turns if needed

- **Professional Support:**
  - Doula with massage training
  - Massage therapist
  - Acupressure points
  - Aromatherapy integration

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces pain perception
- Enhances relaxation
- Improves comfort
- May reduce need for medical pain relief
- Supports emotional well-being
- Enhances birth experience

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Avoid certain areas:** Don't massage ankles (blood clot risk)
- **Use appropriate pressure:** Adjust to comfort
- **Allergies:** Check for oil allergies
- **Communication:** Ask what feels good
- **Practice beforehand:** Learn techniques during pregnancy
