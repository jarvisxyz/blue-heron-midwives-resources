## Summary

This resource covers the use of water for pain management during labour and the option of water birth.

### Key Points

- **Hydrotherapy Benefits:**
  - Warm water reduces pain
  - Buoyancy supports movement
  - Helps relax muscles
  - Improves comfort
  - May reduce labour duration

- **Tub vs. Pool:**
  - **Birthing tub:** Smaller, home use
  - **Birth pool:** Larger, birth centre/hospital
  - **Temperature:** 35-37°C (95-98°F)
  - **Safety:** Clean, well-maintained

- **When to Use Water:**
  - Early labour for pain relief
  - Active labour if available
  - Second stage (birth) in pool
  - Not recommended for all pregnancies

- **Water Birth Considerations:**
  - Eligibility: Low-risk pregnancy
  - Training required for provider
  - Equipment needs
  - Transfer planning if needed
  - Baby's first breath timing

- **Safety Guidelines:**
  - Monitor water temperature
  - Keep water clean
  - Limit time in water
  - Know when to exit water
  - Follow provider guidelines

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces pain and medication needs
- Enhances comfort during labour
- May reduce perineal trauma
- Supports physiological birth
- Improves satisfaction
- Calming effect

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Not for all pregnancies:** High-risk may not qualify
- **Temperature safety:** Don't overheat
- **Hygiene:** Ensure water is clean
- **Monitoring:** Fetal heart monitoring may be limited
- **Transfer planning:** Have backup if needed
- **Consult midwife:** Ensure eligibility
