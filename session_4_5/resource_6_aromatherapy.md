## Summary

This resource covers the use of aromatherapy and essential oils during labour and birth, including safe options and precautions.

### Key Points

- **Safe Essential Oils for Labour:**
  - Lavender: Calming, relaxing
  - Lemon: Uplifting, reduces nausea
  - Peppermint: Nausea relief, energizing
  - Clary Sage: May help labour progress (late labour only)
  - Chamomile: Relaxing

- **Methods of Use:**
  - Diffuser
  - Inhalation (personal inhaler)
  - Diluted massage oil
  - Compress
  - Bath (not during labour)

- **Safety Considerations:**
  - Dilute properly (1-2% concentration)
  - Avoid during first trimester
  - Some oils unsafe in labour (clary sage timing)
  - Check for allergies
  - Use quality oils

- **When to Use:**
  - Early labour for relaxation
  - During contractions for comfort
  - For nausea relief
  - For emotional support

- **Professional Guidance:**
  - Consult aromatherapist
  - Discuss with midwife
  - Learn proper dilution
  - Understand contraindications

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces anxiety and stress
- May reduce nausea
- Enhances relaxation
- Creates calming environment
- Supports emotional well-being
- May reduce need for other interventions

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Not all oils are safe:** Research before use
- **Dilution is critical:** Never apply undiluted
- **Timing matters:** Some oils only for late labour
- **Allergies:** Check for sensitivities
- **Quality matters:** Use pure, therapeutic-grade oils
- **Consult midwife:** Ensure appropriate for your pregnancy
