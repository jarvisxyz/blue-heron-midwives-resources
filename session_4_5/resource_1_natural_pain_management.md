## Summary

This resource provides an overview of natural pain management techniques for labour and birth, including evidence-based non-pharmacological options.

### Key Points

- **Non-Pharmacological Methods:**
  - Breathing techniques
  - Movement and position changes
  - Hydrotherapy (water immersion)
  - Massage and touch
  - Heat and cold applications
  - Aromatherapy
  - TENS units
  - Acupressure and acupuncture

- **Benefits of Natural Methods:**
  - No side effects for baby
  - Can be combined with medical pain relief
  - Empowers birthing person
  - Reduces intervention cascade
  - Enhances sense of control

- **Evidence-Based Approaches:**
  - Continuous support (doula, partner)
  - Water immersion for labour
  - Mobility and position changes
  - Breathing and relaxation techniques
  - Massage therapy

- **Combining Methods:**
  - Natural methods can complement epidurals
  - Layer multiple techniques
  - Adapt to labour progression
  - Personalize approach

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces pain and discomfort
- May reduce need for medical interventions
- Enhances birth experience
- Supports physiological labour
- Improves satisfaction with birth

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Individual responses vary:** What works for one may not work for another
- **Practice beforehand:** Learn techniques during pregnancy
- **Flexibility:** Be open to trying different methods
- **Support person:** Partner or doula can help with techniques
- **Consult midwife:** Ensure methods are appropriate for your pregnancy
