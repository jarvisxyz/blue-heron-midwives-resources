## Summary

This resource covers common labour and birth complications, when they may occur, and how they are managed.

### Key Points

- **Common Labour Complications:**
  - **Prolonged labour:** Labour lasting longer than expected
  - **Failure to progress:** Cervix not dilating as expected
  - **Fetal distress:** Baby showing signs of stress
  - **Breech presentation:** Baby not head-down
  - **Shoulder dystocia:** Baby's shoulder stuck after head birth

- **Management Strategies:**
  - **Position changes:** Help baby rotate
  - **Hydration and nutrition:** Maintain energy
  - **Interventions:** When medically necessary
  - **Transfer:** To hospital if needed
  - **Cesarean:** When vaginal birth not safe

- **Fetal Distress:**
  - **Signs:** Abnormal heart rate patterns
  - **Causes:** Cord compression, placental issues
  - **Management:** Position changes, oxygen, possible delivery
  - **Monitoring:** Continuous or intermittent

- **Postpartum Hemorrhage:**
  - **Definition:** Excessive bleeding after birth
  - **Risk factors:** Overdistended uterus, tearing
  - **Management:** Medications, massage, possible surgery
  - **Prevention:** Active management of third stage

- **Emotional Impact:**
  - Birth trauma possible
  - Processing unexpected outcomes
  - Support resources available
  - Counseling if needed

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Prepares for unexpected situations
- Reduces fear of complications
- Enhances informed decision-making
- Supports emotional processing
- Improves understanding of medical care

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Complications are uncommon:** Most births go smoothly
- **Medical care saves lives:** Interventions when needed
- **Emotional support:** Important after unexpected birth
- **Processing:** Take time to understand what happened
- **Resources available:** Counseling and support groups
- **Trust your team:** They work to keep you and baby safe
