# Resource 3: Movement and Position Changes

**Resource Title:** Movement and Position Changes

## Summary

This resource explores how movement and position changes can help manage labour pain and facilitate labour progression.

### Key Points

- **Benefits of Movement:**
  - May shorten labour duration
  - Helps baby descend
  - Reduces pain and discomfort
  - Improves blood flow
  - Enhances comfort

- **Labour Positions:**
  - **Upright:** Walking, standing, leaning
  - **Squatting:** Opens pelvis
  - **Hands and knees:** Helps with back labour
  - **Side-lying:** Resting position
  - **Birth ball:** Rocking and bouncing
  - **Birthing stool:** Supported squat

- **Position Changes:**
  - Change every 20-30 minutes
  - Follow baby's position
  - Listen to your body
  - Try different positions
  - Use props (balls, stools, bars)

- **Walking and Mobility:**
  - Continue walking in early labour
  - Helps labour progress
  - Reduces pain
  - Use support person
  - Take breaks as needed

- **Water and Movement:**
  - Walking in water reduces gravity
  - Hydrotherapy pool
  - Buoyancy supports movement
  - Reduces pain

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces labour duration
- May reduce need for interventions
- Enhances comfort
- Helps baby rotate and descend
- Improves birth outcomes
- Increases satisfaction

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Monitor blood pressure:** If on medication, may need to stay in bed
- **Safety first:** Use support when moving
- **Hydration:** Drink water when moving
- **Energy conservation:** Rest between contractions
- **Birth location:** Check what's available (pool, birth balls)
