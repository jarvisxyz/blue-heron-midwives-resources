# Resource 11: Child Development Services

**Resource Title:** Child Development Services  
**Session:** Community Resources (Resource 11 of 15)

## Summary

This resource covers child development services and early intervention programs available in Waterloo Region.

### Key Points

- **Development Screening:**
  - Routine developmental checks
  - Early detection of delays
  - Referrals to specialists
  - Follow-up assessments
  - Parent education

- **Early Intervention Services:**
  - Physical therapy
  - Occupational therapy
  - Speech-language pathology
  - Child development services
  - Family support

- **How to Access:**
  - Referral from family doctor or pediatrician
  - Self-referral to some programs
  - Public health screening
  - EarlyON connections

- **Cost:**
  - Many services covered by OHIP
  - Some may have fees
  - Sliding scale available
  - Insurance may cover some services

- **Age Range:**
  - Services from birth to school age
  - Early childhood intervention
  - School readiness programs

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Supports healthy child development
- Early detection of concerns
- Provides timely intervention
- Enhances child outcomes
- Supports family functioning
- Promotes long-term health

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Early intervention is key:** Don't wait if concerned
- **Development varies:** Some variation is normal
- **Follow-up:** Attend all scheduled appointments
- **Parent involvement:** Active participation important
- **Resources:** Developmental specialists available
