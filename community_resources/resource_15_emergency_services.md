## Summary

This resource provides information about emergency services available in Waterloo Region for pregnancy, birth, and parenting emergencies.

### Key Points

- **Emergency Contacts:**
  - **911:** Life-threatening emergencies
  - **Waterloo Region Police:** Non-emergency police matters
  - **Grand River Hospital Emergency:** 519-749-5500
  - **Crisis lines:** Mental health emergencies

- **Pregnancy Emergencies:**
  - Severe bleeding
  - Severe headache or vision changes
  - Decreased fetal movement
  - Signs of preterm labour
  - Signs of preeclampsia

- **Newborn Emergencies:**
  - Fever over 38°C (100.4°F)
  - Breathing difficulties
  - Blue or grey color
  - Severe lethargy
  - Poor feeding

- **Mental Health Emergencies:**
  - 988 Suicide Crisis Helpline
  - Crisis text line
  - Emergency department if immediate danger
  - Postpartum crisis support

- **After-Hours Support:**
  - Hospital emergency departments
  - Crisis lines available 24/7
  - After-hours midwife contact
  - Family doctor after-hours line

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Provides emergency care when needed
- Ensures safety of parent and baby
- Reduces risk of complications
- Provides crisis support
- Enhances peace of mind
- Improves emergency response

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **911 for life-threatening emergencies:** Don't delay
- **Save numbers:** Keep emergency contacts accessible
- **Know your options:** Understand when to call 911 vs. other services
- **Crisis lines:** Free and confidential
- **Emergency department:** Go if unable to reach midwife and concerned
- **Resources:** Emergency services available 24/7
