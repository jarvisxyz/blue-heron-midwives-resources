## Summary

This resource addresses emotional and mental health during pregnancy, covering normal emotional changes, when to seek help, and available support resources.

### Key Points

- **Normal Emotional Changes:**
  - Mood swings due to hormonal changes
  - Increased sensitivity
  - Anxiety about birth and parenting
  - Excitement mixed with fear
  - Varying energy levels

- **Managing Emotional Health:**
  - Open communication with partner
  - Maintain social connections
  - Practice self-care regularly
  - Get adequate sleep
  - Engage in enjoyable activities

- **Stress Management:**
  - Mindfulness and meditation
  - Gentle exercise
  - Breathing exercises
  - Journaling
  - Professional counseling if needed

- **When to Seek Help:**
  - Persistent sadness or hopelessness
  - Loss of interest in activities
  - Thoughts of self-harm
  - Inability to function daily
  - Panic attacks
  - Severe anxiety

- **Support Resources:**
  - Midwife counseling
  - Mental health professionals
  - Support groups
  - Helplines and hotlines
  - Online resources

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Good mental health supports fetal development
- Reduces risk of postpartum depression
- Improves birth outcomes
- Enhances parent-infant bonding
- Reduces pregnancy complications
- Improves quality of life

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't ignore mental health:** It's as important as physical health
- **Early intervention** prevents worsening symptoms
- **Mental health conditions** may require medication - discuss with provider
- **Support system** is crucial - don't isolate
- **Postpartum mental health** begins during pregnancy
- **Confidential help available** - reach out if struggling
