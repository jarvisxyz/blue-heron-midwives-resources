# Resource 8: Managing Nausea and Vomiting

## Summary

This resource covers strategies for managing nausea and vomiting during pregnancy, from mild morning sickness to severe hyperemesis gravidarum.

### Key Points

- **Morning Sickness (Mild to Moderate):**
  - Affects 70-80% of pregnancies
  - Typically begins 4-6 weeks, improves by 12-14 weeks
  - Can occur any time of day, not just morning

- **Strategies for Relief:**
  - Eat small, frequent meals (5-6 daily)
  - Keep crackers by bed, eat before getting up
  - Avoid greasy, spicy, or strong-smelling foods
  - Stay hydrated with small sips throughout day
  - Try ginger (tea, candies, supplements)
  - Vitamin B6 supplementation (10-25mg, 3x daily)
  - Acupressure wrist bands

- **Dietary Modifications:**
  - Cold foods often better tolerated than hot
  - High-protein snacks help
  - Avoid empty stomach
  - Try sour candies or lemon

- **When to Contact Midwife:**
  - Unable to keep fluids down for 24 hours
  - Losing weight
  - Signs of dehydration (dark urine, dizziness)
  - Vomiting blood
  - Symptoms persisting beyond 20 weeks

- **Hyperemesis Gravidarum (Severe):**
  - Affects 1-3% of pregnancies
  - Requires medical treatment
  - May need IV fluids, prescription medications
  - Hospitalization sometimes necessary

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Proper management ensures adequate nutrition
- Prevents dehydration and electrolyte imbalance
- Reduces stress on mother and baby
- May reduce risk of preterm birth
- Improves quality of life during pregnancy

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't ignore severe symptoms** - can indicate hyperemesis
- **Some medications are safe** during pregnancy - consult midwife
- **Dehydration is serious** - seek help if concerned
- **Most cases resolve** by second trimester
- **Individual responses vary** - what works for one may not work for another
