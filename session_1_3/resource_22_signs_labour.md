# Resource 22: Signs of Labour

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-1-3.html  
**Resource Title:** Signs of Labour  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 22 of 40)

## Summary

This resource helps identify the signs that labour is beginning, when to contact your midwife, and when to go to your birth location.

### Key Points

- **True Labour Signs:**
  - **Contractions:** Regular, intensify with movement, don't stop with rest
  - **Pattern:** Become closer together, longer, stronger
  - **Cervical change:** Dilation and effacement
  - **Baby dropping:** Lightening (may happen weeks before)
  - **Energy surge:** Nesting instinct

- **False Labour (Braxton Hicks):**
  - Irregular contractions
  - Stop with movement or rest
  - Don't intensify over time
  - Felt mostly in front, not back
  - No cervical change

- **When to Call Midwife:**
  - Contractions 5 minutes apart, lasting 1 minute, for 1 hour
  - Water breaks (any amount)
  - Decreased fetal movement
  - Vaginal bleeding (more than spotting)
  - Severe headache, vision changes
  - Concern about baby's health

- **When to Go to Birth Location:**
  - Follow midwife's instructions
  - Usually when contractions are regular and strong
  - If water breaks and you're uncomfortable
  - If you feel you can't cope at home
  - For high-risk pregnancies, follow specific guidelines

- **What to Do When Labour Starts:**
  - Time contractions
  - Eat light, digestible food
  - Rest between contractions
  - Stay hydrated
  - Take warm shower if helpful
  - Use comfort measures

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Helps recognize when labour is real
- Reduces anxiety about timing
- Prevents going to birth location too early
- Supports informed decision-making
- Reduces unnecessary interventions
- Empowers parents during labour

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Call midwife first** before going to birth location
- **Water breaking:** Note time, colour, amount
- **Bleeding:** Contact midwife immediately if more than spotting
- **Don't eat heavy meals** once active labour begins
- **Trust your instincts:** You know your body best
- **Have backup plan:** What if midwife unavailable?
