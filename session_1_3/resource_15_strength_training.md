# Resource 15: Strength Training Modifications

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-1-3.html  
**Resource Title:** Strength Training Modifications  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 15 of 40)

## Summary

This resource provides guidance on maintaining strength training during pregnancy with appropriate modifications for each trimester.

### Key Points

- **General Principles:**
  - Maintain muscle mass throughout pregnancy
  - Use lighter weights, higher repetitions
  - Focus on form over weight
  - Modify exercises as body changes
  - Continue if already strength training

- **Upper Body Exercises:**
  - Light dumbbells (5-15 lbs)
  - Resistance bands
  - Bodyweight exercises
  - Avoid heavy overhead pressing
  - Modify range of motion

- **Lower Body Exercises:**
  - Squats (modified depth)
  - Lunges (with support)
  - Calf raises
  - Glute bridges
  - Avoid heavy leg press

- **Core Modifications:**
  - Avoid traditional crunches after first trimester
  - Focus on transverse abdominis
  - Modified planks (on knees)
  - Pelvic tilts
  - Avoid exercises causing coning

- **Exercises to Avoid:**
  - Heavy deadlifts and squats
  - Exercises with fall risk
  - Contact sports
  - Exercises lying flat on back (after 16 weeks)
  - High-impact movements

- **Progression Guidelines:**
  - First trimester: Continue with modifications
  - Second trimester: Adjust for belly growth
  - Third trimester: Focus on maintenance
  - Postpartum: Gradual return

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Maintains muscle strength for labour
- Reduces back pain
- Improves posture
- Prepares body for carrying baby
- Aids postpartum recovery
- Reduces risk of injury
- Maintains metabolic health

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't lift to failure:** Leave 2-3 reps in reserve
- **Breathe properly:** Exhale on exertion
- **Modify as needed:** Comfort over intensity
- **Consult midwife** before continuing strength training
- **Stop if:** Pain, dizziness, contractions, bleeding
- **Focus on functional strength** for daily activities
