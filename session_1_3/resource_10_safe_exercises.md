# Resource 10: Safe Exercises for Pregnancy

**Resource Title:** Safe Exercises for Pregnancy  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 10 of 40)

## Summary

This resource provides detailed information about specific exercises that are safe and beneficial during pregnancy, with modifications for each trimester.

### Key Points

- **Walking:**
  - Most accessible exercise
  - Can continue throughout pregnancy
  - Start with 10-15 minutes, build to 30 minutes
  - Good cardiovascular exercise
  - Low impact on joints

- **Swimming and Water Aerobics:**
  - Excellent for all trimesters
  - Reduces joint stress
  - Helps with swelling
  - Cool water prevents overheating
  - Buoyancy supports growing belly

- **Prenatal Yoga:**
  - Improves flexibility and strength
  - Teaches breathing techniques
  - Reduces stress and anxiety
  - Modifications available for all trimesters
  - Focus on pelvic floor awareness

- **Prenatal Pilates:**
  - Strengthens core (modified)
  - Improves posture
  - Enhances body awareness
  - Pelvic floor exercises included
  - Reduces back pain

- **Strength Training:**
  - Light weights, higher repetitions
  - Focus on upper body and legs
  - Avoid heavy lifting
  - Modify exercises as pregnancy progresses
  - Maintain muscle mass

- **Stationary Cycling:**
  - Low impact cardio
  - Good for all trimesters
  - Indoor cycling reduces fall risk
  - Adjust resistance as needed

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Prepares body for labour demands
- Reduces pregnancy discomforts
- Improves sleep quality
- Reduces risk of gestational diabetes
- Enhances mood and mental health
- May reduce labour duration
- Improves postpartum recovery

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Avoid:** Contact sports, high fall-risk activities, hot yoga
- **Modify:** Balance exercises after first trimester
- **Stop:** If experiencing any warning signs
- **Hydrate:** Drink water before, during, after exercise
- **Warm up and cool down:** 5-10 minutes each
- **Consult midwife** before starting new exercise program
