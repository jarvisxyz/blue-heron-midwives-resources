# Resource 6: Weight Gain Recommendations

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-1-3.html  
**Resource Title:** Weight Gain Recommendations  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 6 of 40)

## Summary

This resource provides evidence-based weight gain recommendations during pregnancy based on pre-pregnancy BMI, following guidelines from Health Canada and the Institute of Medicine.

### Key Points

- **Pre-Pregnancy BMI Categories:**
  - Underweight (BMI < 18.5): Gain 28-40 lbs (12.5-18 kg)
  - Normal weight (BMI 18.5-24.9): Gain 25-35 lbs (11.5-16 kg)
  - Overweight (BMI 25-29.9): Gain 15-25 lbs (7-11.5 kg)
  - Obese (BMI ≥ 30): Gain 11-20 lbs (5-9 kg)

- **Weight Gain Timeline:**
  - First trimester: 1-4 lbs total (minimal gain)
  - Second trimester: ~1 lb/week
  - Third trimester: ~1 lb/week
  - Total recommended gain varies by pre-pregnancy weight

- **Healthy Weight Gain Sources:**
  - Fetal growth: ~7 lbs
  - Placenta: ~1.5 lbs
  - Amniotic fluid: ~2 lbs
  - Increased blood volume: ~4 lbs
  - Breast tissue: ~2 lbs
  - Uterus growth: ~2 lbs
  - Maternal stores: ~7 lbs

- **Monitoring Weight:**
  - Weigh weekly at same time of day
  - Don't obsess over daily fluctuations
  - Focus on overall trend
  - Discuss concerns with midwife

- **When to Be Concerned:**
  - No weight gain by end of first trimester
  - Rapid weight gain (>2 lbs/week after week 20)
  - Sudden swelling in face or hands
  - Weight loss during pregnancy

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Appropriate weight gain reduces risk of:
  - Gestational diabetes
  - Preeclampsia
  - Preterm birth
  - Cesarean section
  - Postpartum weight retention

- Underweight gain risks:
  - Low birth weight
  - Preterm birth
  - Small for gestational age

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't diet** during pregnancy without medical supervision
- **Weight gain is individual** - follow your midwife's guidance
- **Multiple pregnancy** requires different weight gain targets
- **Focus on nutrition quality** rather than weight alone
- **Postpartum weight loss** should be gradual (6-12 months)
