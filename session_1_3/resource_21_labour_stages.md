# Resource 21: Understanding Labour Stages

**Resource Title:** Understanding Labour Stages  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 21 of 40)

## Summary

This resource provides detailed information about the three stages of labour, what to expect during each stage, and how to prepare for the birth experience.

### Key Points

- **First Stage: Dilation**
  - **Early labour:** Contractions 5-20 minutes apart, 30-45 seconds
  - **Active labour:** Contractions 3-5 minutes apart, 45-60 seconds
  - **Transition:** Contractions 2-3 minutes apart, 60-90 seconds
  - **Cervical dilation:** 0-10 centimeters
  - **Duration:** Varies widely, average 12-24 hours for first birth

- **Second Stage: Pushing and Birth**
  - **Full dilation:** 10 centimeters
  - **Pushing:** Baby descends through birth canal
  - **Duration:** 30 minutes to 3 hours
  - **Positions:** Upright, side-lying, squatting, hands-and-knees
  - **Crowning:** Baby's head visible at vaginal opening

- **Third Stage: Placenta Delivery**
  - **Timing:** After baby birth
  - **Duration:** 5-30 minutes
  - **Process:** Placenta separates and delivers
  - **Intervention:** May include uterine massage, cord clamping
  - **Perineal care:** Repair if tearing or episiotomy

- **Signs Labour is Beginning:**
  - Regular contractions that intensify
  - Water breaking (rupture of membranes)
  - Bloody show (mucus plug)
  - Back pain, cramping
  - Diarrhea, nausea

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Understanding stages reduces anxiety
- Helps recognize when to go to birth location
- Prepares for different labour experiences
- Informs birth plan decisions
- Reduces fear of the unknown
- Supports informed decision-making

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Each labour is unique:** Your experience may differ
- **Call midwife** when contractions are regular and 5 minutes apart
- **Water breaking:** Contact midwife immediately
- **Don't drive yourself** to birth location
- **Trust your body:** Labour progresses naturally
- **Flexibility:** Birth plans may need adjustment
