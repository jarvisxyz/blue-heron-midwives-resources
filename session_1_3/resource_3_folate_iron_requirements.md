# Resource 3: Folate and Iron Requirements

## Summary

This resource details the critical importance of folate and iron during pregnancy, with specific recommendations from Health Canada and the College of Midwives of Ontario.

### Key Points

- **Folate (Vitamin B9):**
  - **Recommended intake:** 400-600 mcg daily
  - **Critical period:** First 28 days (often before pregnancy is known)
  - **Prevents:** Neural tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly)
  - **Food sources:** Leafy greens, legumes, fortified grains, citrus fruits
  - **Supplementation:** 400 mcg daily starting before conception

- **Iron:**
  - **Recommended intake:** 27mg daily during pregnancy
  - **Why important:** Supports increased blood volume, prevents anemia
  - **Food sources:** Red meat, spinach, lentils, fortified cereals
  - **Absorption tip:** Take with vitamin C (orange juice) for better absorption
  - **Side effects:** Constipation, dark stools (normal)

- **Folate-Rich Foods (100mcg per serving):**
  - 1/2 cup cooked lentils
  - 1 cup cooked spinach
  - 1/2 cup black beans
  - 1 slice whole wheat bread

- **Iron-Rich Foods (3-5mg per serving):**
  - 3oz lean beef
  - 1 cup cooked spinach
  - 1/2 cup cooked lentils
  - 3oz chicken thigh

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Folate prevents serious birth defects
- Iron prevents maternal anemia and fatigue
- Both support healthy placental development
- Adequate levels reduce risk of preterm birth
- Support healthy blood volume expansion

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Start folate supplementation immediately** if planning pregnancy
- **High-risk groups** may need higher doses (previous NTD pregnancy, certain medications)
- **Iron supplements** may cause constipation - increase fiber and water
- **Don't take iron with calcium** supplements (reduces absorption)
- **Consult your midwife** before changing supplement doses
