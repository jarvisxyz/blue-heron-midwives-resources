## Summary

This resource provides guidance for pregnancies with multiples (twins, triplets, or more), including increased monitoring and special considerations.

### Key Points

- **Types of Multiple Pregnancies:**
  - **Dizygotic (fraternal):** Two separate eggs
  - **Monozygotic (identical):** One egg splits
  - **Monochorionic:** Share placenta (higher risk)
  - **Dichorionic:** Separate placentas

- **Increased Monitoring:**
  - More frequent ultrasounds
  - Additional growth scans
  - Specialized fetal monitoring
  - More frequent appointments
  - Early and ongoing screening

- **Nutritional Needs:**
  - Increased caloric intake (+600-1000 calories)
  - Higher protein requirements
  - Increased iron and folate
  - More calcium and vitamin D
  - Work with dietitian

- **Common Concerns:**
  - Preterm labour (higher risk)
  - Gestational diabetes
  - Preeclampsia
  - Anemia
  - Back pain and discomfort
  - Breathing difficulties

- **Birth Planning:**
  - Hospital birth recommended
  - May deliver earlier (37-38 weeks for twins)
  - C-section may be indicated
  - Specialized neonatal care
  - Recovery may take longer

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces risk of complications
- Ensures appropriate monitoring
- Supports healthy fetal development
- Prepares for potential interventions
- Enhances safety for all babies
- Improves outcomes

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Preterm labour risk:** Higher than singleton pregnancy
- **Follow monitoring schedule:** Critical for safety
- **Nutrition is crucial:** Don't restrict calories
- **Know warning signs:** When to seek immediate care
- **Emotional support:** Multiple pregnancies are challenging
- **Resources available:** Support groups for parents of multiples
