# Resource 11: Pelvic Floor Exercises

**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 11 of 40)

## Summary

This resource covers the importance of pelvic floor health during pregnancy, including Kegel exercises and other pelvic floor strengthening techniques.

### Key Points

- **What is the Pelvic Floor?**
  - Group of muscles supporting bladder, uterus, rectum
  - Extends from pubic bone to tailbone
  - Important for continence and organ support

- **Why Strengthen During Pregnancy:**
  - Supports growing uterus
  - Reduces risk of urinary incontinence
  - Prepares muscles for labour
  - May reduce perineal tearing
  - Aids postpartum recovery

- **Kegel Exercises:**
  - Identify muscles: Stop urine flow mid-stream (once to identify)
  - Contract pelvic floor muscles
  - Hold for 3-5 seconds
  - Relax for 3-5 seconds
  - Repeat 10-15 times, 3x daily
  - Don't hold breath or tighten other muscles

- **Advanced Techniques:**
  - Quick flicks: Rapid contractions
  - Long holds: 10-second contractions
  - Progressive loading: Add resistance
  - Functional integration: During daily activities

- **Pelvic Floor Physical Therapy:**
  - Available if experiencing pain or dysfunction
  - Biofeedback available
  - Individualized treatment plans
  - Can address diastasis recti

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces urinary incontinence during and after pregnancy
- May reduce risk of pelvic organ prolapse
- Prepares muscles for pushing during labour
- May reduce perineal trauma
- Aids postpartum recovery
- Improves sexual function postpartum

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't overdo it:** Can cause muscle tightness
- **Relaxation is equally important:** Practice letting go
- **If pain occurs:** Consult pelvic floor therapist
- **Timing:** Start early, continue throughout pregnancy
- **Don't practice while urinating** (except once to identify muscles)
- **Consistency:** Daily practice yields best results
