# Resource 9: Exercise Safety Guidelines

**Resource Title:** Exercise Safety Guidelines  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 9 of 40)

## Summary

This resource outlines safe exercise practices during pregnancy, including contraindications, modifications, and recommendations from Canadian health authorities.

### Key Points

- **General Recommendations:**
  - Aim for 150 minutes moderate exercise per week
  - Break into 10-15 minute sessions if needed
  - Listen to your body - modify as needed
  - Stay hydrated and avoid overheating

- **Safe Exercise Intensity:**
  - "Talk test": Should be able to hold conversation
  - Rate of perceived exertion: 5-6 out of 10
  - Heart rate monitoring less reliable during pregnancy
  - Focus on how you feel, not numbers

- **Contraindications (Don't Exercise If):**
  - Vaginal bleeding
  - Ruptured membranes
  - Placenta previa after 26 weeks
  - Preterm labour
  - Preeclampsia
  - Severe anemia
  - Certain heart or lung conditions

- **Warning Signs to Stop:**
  - Vaginal bleeding or fluid leakage
  - Dizziness or faintness
  - Chest pain
  - Headache
  - Muscle weakness
  - Calf pain or swelling
  - Regular painful contractions

- **Exercise Modifications:**
  - Avoid contact sports
  - No scuba diving or skydiving
  - Avoid exercises with fall risk
  - Modify balance exercises after first trimester
  - Avoid lying flat on back after 16 weeks

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces risk of gestational diabetes
- Lowers risk of preeclampsia
- Reduces back pain and discomfort
- Improves sleep quality
- Reduces stress and anxiety
- May reduce labour duration
- Improves postpartum recovery

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Consult your midwife** before starting new exercise routine
- **Previous exercise level** matters - build gradually if new to exercise
- **Heat and humidity** increase risk of overheating
- **Core exercises** need modification after first trimester
- **Pelvic floor health** is important - include appropriate exercises
