# Resource 14: Swimming and Water Exercise

## Summary

This resource covers swimming and water-based exercises as excellent options for maintaining fitness during pregnancy, with specific benefits and safety considerations.

### Key Points

- **Benefits of Water Exercise:**
  - Buoyancy reduces joint stress
  - Supports growing belly and weight
  - Reduces swelling in legs and feet
  - Helps maintain core temperature
  - Lowers risk of falls
  - Can continue throughout pregnancy

- **Types of Water Exercise:**
  - Swimming (any stroke)
  - Water walking
  - Aqua aerobics
  - Water resistance training
  - Pregnancy water classes

- **Getting Started:**
  - Find prenatal water exercise classes
  - Ensure pool is clean and well-maintained
  - Wear supportive swimwear
  - Shower before and after swimming
  - Stay hydrated even in water

- **Swimming Techniques:**
  - Freestyle and backstroke preferred
  - Modify breaststroke if hip discomfort
  - Avoid diving or jumping in
  - Use kickboard for leg exercises
  - Swim at comfortable pace

- **Water Temperature:**
  - Ideal: 78-82°F (25-28°C)
  - Avoid hot tubs and saunas
  - Don't overheat
  - Take breaks if feeling warm

- **Safety Considerations:**
  - Enter and exit pool carefully
  - Use handrails
  - Don't swim alone
  - Know your limits
  - Shower after swimming

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Excellent cardiovascular exercise
- Reduces pregnancy discomforts
- Helps with swelling and edema
- Maintains fitness without impact
- May reduce risk of gestational diabetes
- Improves sleep quality
- Prepares body for labour

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Avoid:** Hot tubs, saunas, very hot pools
- **Infection risk:** Ensure pool is properly chlorinated
- **Slippery surfaces:** Use caution entering/exiting
- **Don't hold breath:** Can reduce oxygen to baby
- **Listen to body:** Modify intensity as needed
- **Consult midwife** if concerned about water activities
