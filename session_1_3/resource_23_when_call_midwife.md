# Resource 23: When to Call Your Midwife

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-1-3.html  
**Resource Title:** When to Call Your Midwife  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 23 of 40)

## Summary

This resource outlines when and how to contact your midwife during pregnancy, labour, and postpartum period, ensuring timely care and support.

### Key Points

- **During Pregnancy - Call If:**
  - Vaginal bleeding or spotting
  - Leaking fluid from vagina
  - Decreased fetal movement (after 28 weeks)
  - Severe headache or vision changes
  - Severe abdominal pain
  - Fever over 100.4°F (38°C)
  - Signs of preterm labour (before 37 weeks)
  - Severe swelling in face or hands
  - Thoughts of self-harm

- **Labour - Call If:**
  - Contractions 5 minutes apart, lasting 1 minute, for 1 hour
  - Water breaks (note time, colour, amount)
  - Concern about baby's movement
  - Bleeding (more than bloody show)
  - Feeling like you need to push
  - Any concerns or questions

- **Postpartum - Call If:**
  - Heavy bleeding (soaking pad in hour)
  - Fever over 100.4°F (38°C)
  - Severe headache
  - Chest pain or shortness of breath
  - Thoughts of harming self or baby
  - Breast infection signs (red, painful breast)
  - Wound infection (if C-section or tearing)
  - Severe mood changes

- **How to Contact:**
  - Midwife's phone number (24/7 availability)
  - After-hours answering service
  - Emergency department if unable to reach midwife
  - Text or email if offered
  - Follow specific instructions from your midwife

- **Documentation:**
  - Keep midwife's number handy
  - Save in phone contacts
  - Share with partner and support people
  - Know backup contact procedures

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Ensures timely medical attention
- Prevents complications
- Reduces anxiety about when to seek help
- Supports informed decision-making
- Maintains continuity of care
- Provides peace of mind

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Emergency situations:** Call 911 if severe symptoms
- **Don't wait:** Better to call with concerns
- **Trust your instincts:** You know your body best
- **After-hours:** Midwives typically available 24/7
- **Emergency department:** Go if unable to reach midwife and concerned
- **Documentation:** Keep contact information accessible
