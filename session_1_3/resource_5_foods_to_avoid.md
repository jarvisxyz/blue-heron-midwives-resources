# Resource 5: Foods to Avoid During Pregnancy

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-1-3.html  
**Resource Title:** Foods to Avoid During Pregnancy  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 5 of 40)

## Summary

This resource outlines foods and substances that should be avoided or limited during pregnancy due to risks of foodborne illness, developmental harm, or pregnancy complications.

### Key Points

- **High-Risk Foods to Avoid:**
  - Raw or undercooked meats (including rare steaks)
  - Raw fish and shellfish (sushi, sashimi, ceviche)
  - Unpasteurized dairy products (soft cheeses, raw milk)
  - Raw or undercooked eggs
  - Unwashed fruits and vegetables
  - Deli meats and hot dogs (unless heated until steaming)
  - Smoked seafood (unless cooked)

- **High-Mercury Fish to Avoid:**
  - Shark
  - Swordfish
  - King mackerel
  - Tilefish
  - Orange roughy

- **Foods to Limit:**
  - Caffeine: Maximum 200mg/day (1-2 cups coffee)
  - Alcohol: No amount is considered safe
  - Sugary foods and drinks
  - Processed foods high in sodium

- **Food Safety Practices:**
  - Wash hands before food preparation
  - Cook meats to safe internal temperatures
  - Reheat deli meats until steaming
  - Store food at proper temperatures
  - Discard leftovers after 3-4 days

- **Herbal Supplements:**
  - Consult midwife before using any herbal products
  - Some herbs can stimulate contractions
  - Avoid unknown herbal teas

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Prevents listeria, toxoplasmosis, and salmonella infections
- Reduces risk of miscarriage and stillbirth
- Protects fetal brain development (mercury avoidance)
- Reduces risk of gestational diabetes
- Supports healthy weight gain

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Listeria risk:** Can cause serious complications even if mother has mild symptoms
- **Alcohol:** No safe level during pregnancy - complete avoidance recommended
- **Caffeine:** Excessive intake linked to low birth weight
- **Food poisoning:** Can be more severe during pregnancy
- **When in doubt:** Consult your midwife or healthcare provider
