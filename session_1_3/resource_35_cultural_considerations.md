# Resource 35: Cultural Considerations in Care

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-1-3.html  
**Resource Title:** Cultural Considerations in Care  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 35 of 40)

## Summary

This resource addresses cultural considerations in pregnancy and birth care, ensuring culturally safe and respectful care for all families.

### Key Points

- **Cultural Safety:**
  - Respect for cultural beliefs and practices
  - Understanding cultural differences
  - Avoiding assumptions
  - Providing culturally appropriate care
  - Language access and interpretation

- **Indigenous Pregnancy Care:**
  - Traditional healing practices
  - Cultural ceremonies
  - Connection to land and community
  - Specific health considerations
  - Cultural safety training for providers

- **Religious Considerations:**
  - Dietary restrictions
  - Modesty preferences
  - Prayer and spiritual practices
  - Birth timing considerations
  - Postpartum traditions

- **Immigrant and Refugee Considerations:**
  - Language barriers
  - Different health beliefs
  - Access to services
  - Social support needs
  - Navigation of healthcare system

- **LGBTQ2S+ Inclusive Care:**
  - Affirming language
  - Inclusive forms and documentation
  - Support for diverse family structures
  - Specific health considerations
  - Creating welcoming environment

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Improves quality of care
- Reduces discrimination and bias
- Enhances trust in healthcare system
- Supports positive birth experience
- Improves health outcomes
- Promotes equity and inclusion

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Cultural safety is a right:** You deserve respectful care
- **Speak up:** If care feels culturally inappropriate
- **Resources available:** Cultural brokers, interpreters
- **Advocacy:** Always available if needed
- **Documentation:** Keep records of concerns
- **Community support:** Connect with cultural community
