# Resource 1: Nutrition During Pregnancy

**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 1 of 40)

## Summary

Proper nutrition during pregnancy is essential for fetal development and maternal health. This resource covers fundamental nutritional guidelines recommended by Health Canada and the Public Health Agency of Canada.

### Key Points

- **Caloric Needs:** 
  - First trimester: No additional calories needed
  - Second trimester: +340 calories/day
  - Third trimester: +450 calories/day
  - Focus on nutrient-dense foods rather than empty calories

- **Macronutrient Balance:**
  - Protein: 60-70g daily (lean meats, beans, dairy, eggs)
  - Carbohydrates: 175g daily (whole grains, fruits, vegetables)
  - Fats: Include omega-3 fatty acids (DHA) for brain development

- **Essential Nutrients:**
  - Folate: 400-600 mcg daily (leafy greens, fortified grains)
  - Iron: 27mg daily (red meat, spinach, fortified cereals)
  - Calcium: 1000mg daily (dairy, fortified plant milks)
  - Vitamin D: 600 IU daily (sunlight, fortified foods, supplements)

- **Hydration:**
  - 8-10 glasses of water daily
  - Monitor urine color (pale yellow = well-hydrated)
  - Limit caffeine to 200mg/day (approximately 1-2 cups coffee)

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

Adequate nutrition supports:
- Healthy fetal growth and organ development
- Prevention of neural tube defects (folate)
- Prevention of anemia (iron)
- Bone development (calcium, vitamin D)
- Reduced risk of gestational diabetes
- Better labour outcomes

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Avoid:** Raw fish, unpasteurized dairy, undercooked meats, high-mercury fish
- **Limit:** Caffeine, sugary foods, processed foods
- **Consult your midwife** before starting any supplement regimen
- **Individual needs vary** based on pre-pregnancy weight, activity level, and health conditions
