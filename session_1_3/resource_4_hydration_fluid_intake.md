# Resource 4: Hydration and Fluid Intake

## Summary

Proper hydration is essential throughout pregnancy for maternal health, fetal development, and labour preparation. This resource covers fluid intake recommendations and practical hydration strategies.

### Key Points

- **Daily Fluid Requirements:**
  - Minimum: 8-10 glasses (2-2.5 litres) of water daily
  - Increase during exercise, hot weather, or illness
  - Monitor urine color (pale yellow = well-hydrated)

- **Fluid Sources:**
  - Water (primary source)
  - Milk and fortified plant milks
  - Herbal teas (caffeine-free)
  - Soups and broths
  - Fruits and vegetables with high water content

- **Signs of Dehydration:**
  - Dark yellow urine
  - Dry mouth, lips, or skin
  - Headache, dizziness
  - Reduced fetal movement
  - Constipation

- **Hydration Strategies:**
  - Keep water bottle with you at all times
  - Set phone reminders to drink
  - Infuse water with fruit for variety
  - Drink before feeling thirsty
  - Monitor intake during exercise

- **Electrolyte Balance:**
  - Important during hot weather or exercise
  - Natural sources: coconut water, bananas, potatoes
  - Oral rehydration solutions if needed

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Supports increased blood volume (40-50% increase)
- Helps prevent urinary tract infections
- Reduces risk of constipation and hemorrhoids
- Supports amniotic fluid levels
- Prepares body for labour (hydration reduces contraction intensity)
- Reduces risk of preterm contractions

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Avoid:** Excessive caffeine, sugary drinks, alcohol
- **Limit:** Artificial sweeteners
- **Don't overhydrate:** Can lead to electrolyte imbalance
- **Consult your midwife** if experiencing excessive thirst or swelling
- **Monitor** for signs of preeclampsia (sudden swelling, headache)
