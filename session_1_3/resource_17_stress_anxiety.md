# Resource 17: Managing Stress and Anxiety

## Summary

This resource provides practical strategies for managing stress and anxiety during pregnancy, with evidence-based techniques and when to seek professional help.

### Key Points

- **Common Stressors:**
  - Financial concerns
  - Relationship changes
  - Work-life balance
  - Health of baby
  - Preparation for parenting
  - Body image changes

- **Relaxation Techniques:**
  - Deep breathing exercises
  - Progressive muscle relaxation
  - Guided imagery
  - Meditation and mindfulness
  - Warm baths (not hot)

- **Lifestyle Modifications:**
  - Regular sleep schedule
  - Balanced nutrition
  - Gentle exercise
  - Limit caffeine
  - Reduce screen time before bed

- **Cognitive Strategies:**
  - Challenge negative thoughts
  - Practice gratitude
  - Focus on what you can control
  - Set realistic expectations
  - Celebrate small wins

- **Social Support:**
  - Talk to trusted friends
  - Join pregnancy groups
  - Attend birth classes
  - Connect with other parents
  - Share feelings openly

- **Professional Help:**
  - Counseling or therapy
  - Cognitive behavioral therapy
  - Support groups
  - Medication if necessary (discuss with provider)

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces risk of preterm birth
- Lowers blood pressure
- Improves sleep quality
- Reduces pregnancy complications
- Enhances parent-infant bonding
- Reduces risk of postpartum depression
- Improves overall pregnancy experience

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Chronic stress** can affect pregnancy outcomes
- **Don't minimize feelings:** Anxiety is common and treatable
- **Seek help early** if symptoms persist
- **Some medications are safe** - discuss with provider
- **Self-care is not selfish:** Essential for pregnancy health
- **Resources available:** Don't struggle alone
