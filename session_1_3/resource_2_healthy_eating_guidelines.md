## Summary

This resource provides practical, actionable guidelines for maintaining healthy eating patterns throughout pregnancy, aligned with Canadian dietary recommendations.

### Key Points

- **Canada's Food Guide for Pregnancy:**
  - Vegetables and fruits: 7-10 servings daily (varied colours)
  - Whole grains: 3-7 servings daily (oats, brown rice, whole wheat)
  - Protein foods: 2-3 servings daily (beans, fish, poultry, eggs)
  - Milk and alternatives: 2-3 servings daily (milk, yogurt, cheese)

- **Meal Planning Strategies:**
  - Plan meals 1 week in advance
  - Batch cook on weekends
  - Keep healthy snacks accessible
  - Prepare for nausea with small, frequent meals

- **Snack Recommendations:**
  - Apple slices with peanut butter
  - Yogurt with berries
  - Nuts and seeds
  - Whole grain crackers with hummus
  - Hard-boiled eggs

- **Eating Out During Pregnancy:**
  - Choose restaurants with fresh, whole foods
  - Ask about food preparation methods
  - Avoid buffets and salad bars
  - Ensure meats are well-cooked

- **Budget-Friendly Options:**
  - Buy frozen vegetables (nutrient-rich, affordable)
  - Purchase store-brand items
  - Use seasonal produce
  - Take advantage of community food programs

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

Healthy eating patterns:
- Support steady weight gain
- Reduce risk of gestational diabetes
- Improve energy levels
- Support fetal brain development
- Reduce pregnancy complications
- Prepare body for labour and breastfeeding

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Food safety is critical:** Avoid listeria risks (unwashed produce, deli meats unless heated)
- **Listen to your body:** Hunger and appetite fluctuate
- **Don't restrict calories** without medical supervision
- **Individual dietary needs** may vary based on cultural preferences, allergies, or medical conditions
