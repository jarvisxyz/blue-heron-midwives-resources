# Resource 7: Prenatal Vitamins Guide

## Summary

This resource provides comprehensive information about prenatal vitamin supplementation, including what to look for, timing, and special considerations.

### Key Points

- **Essential Components:**
  - Folate/Folic Acid: 400-800 mcg daily
  - Iron: 27mg daily
  - Calcium: 200-300mg (most needs come from diet)
  - Vitamin D: 400-600 IU daily
  - DHA (Omega-3): 200-300mg daily
  - Iodine: 150mcg daily
  - B12: 2.6mcg daily (important for vegetarians/vegans)

- **When to Start:**
  - Ideally 1-3 months before conception
  - At confirmation of pregnancy if not already taking
  - Continue throughout pregnancy and breastfeeding

- **Types of Prenatal Vitamins:**
  - Standard prenatal (most common)
  - Iron-free options (if anemic concerns)
  - Vegetarian/vegan formulations
  - Gummy versions (often lack iron)
  - Prescription-strength options

- **Taking Prenatal Vitamins:**
  - Take with food to reduce nausea
  - Morning or bedtime (whichever works best)
  - Consistency is key - take daily
  - Don't skip doses

- **Common Side Effects:**
  - Nausea (take with food, try different brand)
  - Constipation (increase fiber, water)
  - Dark stools (normal with iron)
  - Metallic taste (temporary)

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Prevents neural tube defects (folate)
- Prevents iron-deficiency anemia
- Supports fetal bone development (calcium, vitamin D)
- Supports brain development (DHA, iodine)
- Reduces risk of preterm birth
- Supports maternal health

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't exceed recommended doses** - some vitamins can be harmful in excess
- **Vitamin A:** Avoid >10,000 IU daily (can cause birth defects)
- **Iron supplements** can interact with certain medications
- **Gummy vitamins** often lack iron - may need separate supplement
- **Consult your midwife** before changing supplement regimen
- **Store** out of reach of children (iron overdose is dangerous)
