# Resource 24: Birth Plan Development

**Resource Title:** Birth Plan Development  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 24 of 40)

## Summary

This resource guides the creation of a birth plan that communicates preferences and preferences for labour and birth while maintaining flexibility.

### Key Points

- **What is a Birth Plan?**
  - Document outlining preferences for labour and birth
  - Communication tool for healthcare team
  - Not a contract - can change during labour
  - Helps partners understand preferences
  - Should be flexible and adaptable

- **Key Sections:**
  - **Preferences for labour:** Movement, positions, food/drink
  - **Pain management:** Preferences for medications and non-pharmacological methods
  - **Monitoring:** Frequency of fetal heart monitoring
  - **Interventions:** Preferences for induction, augmentation, episiotomy
  - **Birth environment:** Lighting, music, who's present
  - **Immediate postpartum:** Skin-to-skin, delayed cord clamping, breastfeeding

- **Questions to Consider:**
  - Where do you want to give birth?
  - Who do you want present?
  - What comfort measures do you prefer?
  - What are your views on interventions?
  - What are your preferences for the baby's care?
  - What are your cultural or religious preferences?

- **Making Your Plan:**
  - Research options early
  - Discuss with midwife
  - Keep it concise (1-2 pages)
  - Prioritize preferences
  - Share with birth team

- **Flexibility:**
  - Birth may not go as planned
  - Medical needs may require changes
  - Stay open to new information
  - Focus on healthy outcome over specific plan

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Improves communication with healthcare team
- Reduces anxiety about birth
- Helps partners understand preferences
- May reduce unnecessary interventions
- Supports informed decision-making
- Enhances birth experience

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Birth plans are not guarantees:** Medical needs come first
- **Be flexible:** Labour is unpredictable
- **Discuss with midwife:** Ensure preferences are feasible
- **Share copies:** With birth team and hospital
- **Focus on priorities:** Don't get stuck on details
- **Revisit and update:** As pregnancy progresses
