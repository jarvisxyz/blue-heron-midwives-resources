# Resource 19: Building Support Networks

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-1-3.html  
**Resource Title:** Building Support Networks  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 19 of 40)

## Summary

This resource provides guidance on building and maintaining support networks during pregnancy and postpartum, recognizing the importance of social connections for maternal and infant health.

### Key Points

- **Types of Support:**
  - Emotional support (listening, encouragement)
  - Practical support (meals, childcare, errands)
  - Informational support (advice, resources)
  - Financial support (if needed)
  - Physical support (help with tasks)

- **Building Your Network:**
  - Identify supportive people early
  - Be specific about needs
  - Accept help when offered
  - Join pregnancy and parenting groups
  - Connect with other expecting parents

- **Key Support People:**
  - Partner/spouse
  - Family members
  - Close friends
  - Other parents
  - Healthcare providers
  - Community resources

- **Communication Strategies:**
  - Express needs clearly
  - Don't hesitate to ask for help
  - Set boundaries when needed
  - Be open about feelings
  - Regular check-ins with supporters

- **Community Resources:**
  - Parent groups and classes
  - Community centers
  - Online forums and groups
  - Hospital programs
  - Non-profit organizations

- **Digital Support:**
  - Parenting apps and forums
  - Social media groups
  - Video calls with distant family
  - Online counseling resources

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces isolation and loneliness
- Lowers risk of postpartum depression
- Improves birth outcomes
- Enhances parenting confidence
- Reduces stress and anxiety
- Provides practical assistance
- Improves overall well-being

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Quality over quantity:** Few supportive people better than many unsupportive
- **Don't isolate:** Reach out even when feeling overwhelmed
- **Mental health:** Support network is crucial for prevention
- **Be selective:** Not all advice is helpful - trust your instincts
- **Reciprocity:** Support flows both ways
- **Professional help:** Sometimes needed in addition to informal support
