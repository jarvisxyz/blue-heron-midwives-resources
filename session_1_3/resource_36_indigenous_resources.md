# Resource 36: Indigenous Pregnancy Resources

**Source URL:** https://bhmconnectingpregnancy.weebly.com/session-1-3.html  
**Resource Title:** Indigenous Pregnancy Resources  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 36 of 40)

## Summary

This resource provides information and resources specifically for Indigenous pregnant individuals, including traditional healing practices and culturally appropriate care.

### Key Points

- **Traditional Healing Practices:**
  - Connection to land and nature
  - Traditional medicines
  - Ceremonial practices
  - Community support
  - Elders and knowledge keepers

- **Culturally Safe Care:**
  - Indigenous health workers
  - Cultural brokers
  - Traditional birth attendants
  - Integration of Western and traditional medicine
  - Respect for cultural practices

- **Specific Health Considerations:**
  - Higher risk of gestational diabetes
  - Increased monitoring recommended
  - Social determinants of health
  - Access to services
  - Historical trauma considerations

- **Support Resources:**
  - Indigenous Health Centers
  - Aboriginal Health Organizations
  - Traditional healing programs
  - Community support groups
  - Cultural ceremonies

- **Rights and Resources:**
  - Non-Insured Health Benefits (NIHB)
  - Travel assistance for care
  - Cultural safety training for providers
  - Indigenous-led programs
  - Community-based care

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Improves pregnancy outcomes
- Reduces barriers to care
- Supports cultural identity
- Enhances family and community connections
- Promotes holistic health
- Reduces health disparities

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Cultural safety is essential:** You deserve respectful care
- **Traditional practices are valid:** Integrate with medical care
- **Support available:** Indigenous health workers
- **Community resources:** Connect with Indigenous organizations
- **Advocacy:** Always available if needed
- **Healing:** Address historical trauma with support
