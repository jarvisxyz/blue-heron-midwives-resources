## Summary

This resource addresses pregnancy considerations related to maternal age, including pregnancies before 20 and after 35.

### Key Points

- **Teenage Pregnancies (<20 years):**
  - **Risks:** Preterm birth, low birth weight, anemia
  - **Considerations:** Continued education, social support
  - **Care:** More frequent monitoring
  - **Support:** Family involvement, social services

- **Advanced Maternal Age (35+):**
  - **Increased risks:** Chromosomal abnormalities, gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, C-section
  - **Screening options:** NIPT, amniocentesis, CVS
  - **Monitoring:** More frequent appointments
  - **Preparation:** Early planning for birth

- **Very Advanced Maternal Age (40+):**
  - **Higher risks:** All above plus stillbirth, miscarriage
  - **Enhanced monitoring:** Additional testing
  - **Discussion:** Birth timing, delivery options
  - **Support:** Emotional and practical

- **Fertility Considerations:**
  - May require fertility treatments
  - Higher risk of multiples with treatments
  - More monitoring during pregnancy
  - Emotional support important

- **Health Optimization:**
  - Pre-conception health check
  - Manage chronic conditions
  - Healthy lifestyle
  - Appropriate weight

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Improves pregnancy outcomes
- Reduces risk of complications
- Ensures appropriate screening
- Supports informed decision-making
- Enhances safety for parent and baby
- Provides emotional support

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Don't panic:** Many healthy pregnancies at any age
- **Screening is optional:** Discuss with provider
- **Age is just one factor:** Overall health matters
- **Early prenatal care:** Critical for all ages
- **Emotional support:** Age-related concerns are common
- **Resources available:** Support groups and counseling
