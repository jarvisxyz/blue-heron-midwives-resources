# Resource 12: Walking and Cardiovascular Health

**Resource Title:** Walking and Cardiovascular Health  
**Session:** Session 1-3 (Resource 12 of 40)

## Summary

This resource focuses on walking as an accessible, safe form of cardiovascular exercise during pregnancy, with practical tips for maintaining an active lifestyle.

### Key Points

- **Benefits of Walking:**
  - Improves cardiovascular health
  - Maintains fitness without high impact
  - Can be done anywhere, anytime
  - No equipment needed
  - Safe throughout all trimesters

- **Getting Started:**
  - Start with 10-15 minute walks
  - Build to 30 minutes daily
  - Walk at comfortable pace
  - Use supportive footwear
  - Walk on even surfaces

- **Walking Progression:**
  - Week 1-4: 10-15 minutes, 3x weekly
  - Week 5-8: 15-20 minutes, 4x weekly
  - Week 9+: 20-30 minutes, 5x weekly
  - Adjust based on comfort and energy

- **Walking Techniques:**
  - Good posture: Head up, shoulders back
  - Engage core gently
  - Swing arms naturally
  - Land mid-foot, not heel
  - Take regular breaks if needed

- **Walking Groups and Programs:**
  - Prenatal walking groups
  - Community programs
  - Walking with friends for motivation
  - Track progress with pedometer or app

- **Weather Considerations:**
  - Hot weather: Walk early morning or evening
  - Cold weather: Layer clothing, stay dry
  - Rain: Indoor walking options (malls, stairs)
  - Air quality: Check air quality index

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces risk of gestational diabetes
- Maintains healthy weight gain
- Improves sleep quality
- Reduces stress and anxiety
- Prepares body for labour
- May reduce risk of preeclampsia
- Improves circulation and reduces swelling

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Stop if:** Dizziness, chest pain, contractions, bleeding
- **Hydrate:** Drink water before and during walks
- **Sun protection:** Wear hat, sunscreen
- **Listen to body:** Modify intensity as needed
- **Consult midwife** if new to exercise or have concerns
