## Summary

This resource explores prenatal yoga as a safe and beneficial practice during pregnancy, covering poses, breathing techniques, and modifications for each trimester.

### Key Points

- **Benefits of Prenatal Yoga:**
  - Reduces stress and anxiety
  - Improves flexibility and strength
  - Teaches breathing for labour
  - Reduces back pain and discomfort
  - Connects mind and body
  - Prepares for birth and parenting

- **Safe Poses (First Trimester):**
  - Mountain pose
  - Warrior I and II (modified)
  - Tree pose (with support)
  - Cat-cow stretch
  - Child's pose (wide-kneed)

- **Safe Poses (Second/Third Trimester):**
  - Modified warrior poses
  - Side-lying poses
  - Seated forward folds
  - Hip openers
  - Pelvic tilts

- **Breathing Techniques:**
  - Diaphragmatic breathing
  - Labour breathing patterns
  - Relaxation techniques
  - Mindfulness practices

- **What to Avoid:**
  - Deep twists (compress abdomen)
  - Lying flat on back (after 16 weeks)
  - Deep backbends
  - Hot yoga (overheating risk)
  - Inversions without experience

- **Yoga Props:**
  - Blocks for support
  - Bolsters for comfort
  - Blankets for cushioning
  - Chairs for seated poses

### Relevance to Pregnancy/Birth

- Reduces pregnancy discomforts
- Prepares body for labour positions
- Teaches breathing for pain management
- Reduces stress and anxiety
- May reduce risk of preterm labour
- Improves sleep quality
- Connects with baby through practice

### Warnings and Important Notes

- **Find prenatal-specific classes** - not all yoga is pregnancy-safe
- **Inform instructor** of pregnancy and trimester
- **Modify as needed** - comfort over flexibility
- **Don't force poses** - body changes during pregnancy
- **Stay hydrated** and avoid overheating
- **Consult midwife** before starting yoga practice
